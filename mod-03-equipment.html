The machines around the machine.

The boiler gets the attention, but gas doesn't move and water doesn't pressurise without fans, pumps, and the instruments quietly watching both.

Moving the water

Centrifugal vs. Positive Displacement.

CentrifugalPositive Displacement
Flow behaviourFlow varies with system back-pressure — follows a head-flow curveDelivers near-constant flow regardless of downstream pressure
Typical use hereBoiler feed pump (multistage, built for 69 kg/cm² delivery), cooling water, general transferChemical dosing, metering pumps, reciprocating plunger pumps for precise low-flow high-pressure jobs
Needs a relief valve?No — flow self-limits against a closed valveYes — a blocked discharge with no relief can burst the line

A reciprocating pump is the classic positive-displacement design: a piston or plunger drawing fluid in on the suction stroke and forcing a fixed volume out on the discharge stroke. That fixed volume per stroke is exactly why its flow doesn't care what the discharge pressure is doing — and exactly why it always needs a relief valve on the discharge side.

Watching the process

Instrumentation.

Vibrating Fork Level Switch

A tuning fork vibrates at its natural frequency in air. When liquid covers it, the frequency damps measurably — that shift triggers the switch. No moving parts beyond the fork itself, reliable for high/low point-level alarms and pump dry-run protection.

O₂ Analyser

In-situ probe in the flue gas path measuring residual oxygen. Tells you how much excess air is going through — too little means incomplete combustion risk, too much means heat is being thrown away heating unnecessary air.

Scrubber

Wet gas-cleaning stage downstream of the boiler. Sprays liquid through the gas stream to capture particulate before it reaches the ID fan and stack, protecting the fan and meeting emission limits.

Quick check

Q&A — Equipment

Why does a positive displacement pump need a relief valve but a centrifugal pump doesn't? SAFETY
A centrifugal pump's flow drops toward zero as back-pressure rises toward shutoff head — it self-limits. A PD pump keeps trying to push the same fixed volume per stroke regardless of resistance, so a blocked line with no relief path will keep building pressure until something fails.
Why is the ID fan placed downstream of the boiler rather than the FD fan doing all the work? DESIGN
Pulling from downstream keeps the furnace and gas path under slight negative pressure (balanced draft), so any casing leakage draws air in rather than letting hot gas and combustion products leak out into the work area.
What does the O₂ analyser reading actually tell an operator to do? OPERATIONS
It's the feedback signal for air-fuel balance. A falling O₂ trend with stable load suggests trimming combustion air up; a high, climbing O₂ with no load change suggests excess air is being pulled in somewhere, wasting heat up the stack.
